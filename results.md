## Conclusion on Category-Specific RL Performance
Our analysis reveals that RL demonstrates strong performance in subjective quality metrics (Consistency, Grounding, Persona), achieving excellence across most categories, indicating effective optimization for coherence, factual accuracy, and personalization. However, RL shows variability in objective metrics (MAE, Spearman), with notable differences between categories.

Handmade_Products stands out as a preferred category for RL, excelling in all metrics, likely due to its objective and quantifiable review characteristics (e.g., focus on materials, durability), which align well with RL's feedback-based learning. In contrast, CDs_and_Vinyl exhibits deviations, particularly in Spearman and related metrics, despite similar data volume and quality to other categories. This is attributed to the category's inherent subjective and culturally dependent nature (e.g., aesthetic preferences in music), making it challenging for RL to generalize and optimize consistently.

Data statistics confirm that differences are not due to quantity (both categories have 400 entries with ~19 reviews each) or basic quality (CDs_and_Vinyl reviews are even longer on average), but rather category-specific traits. Future work could explore targeted adaptations, such as category-aware reward functions or fine-tuning, to mitigate such variations and enhance RL robustness across diverse product domains.
